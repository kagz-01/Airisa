    2. COMPANY BACKGROUND

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Airisa Green Consulting is a registered social enterprise and sustainability consultancy working at the intersection of sustainable mobility, gender & inclusion, and climate action across emerging markets.

We operate a hybrid model — providing expert consulting services to governments, development partners, and private sector clients, while also designing and implementing impact-driven programs that empower communities, inform policy, and foster inclusive, low-carbon transport systems.

Founded in 2024, Airisa serves as a catalyst for equitable transport transformation across Africa and emerging markets, leveraging data, local knowledge, and global partnerships to drive lasting change.

VISION STATEMENT

To be Africa’s leading consultancy advancing inclusive mobility, climate resilience, and environmental sustainability through research, policy innovation, and community‑centered implementation.

MISSION STATEMENT

To transform mobility systems through research excellence, gender-inclusive advocacy, and collaborative partnerships that bridge the gap between policy and implementation while fostering innovation in emerging markets' transport sectors.


VALUES
    1. Research Excellence: Commitment to rigorous, evidence-based analysis and thought leadership

    3. Innovation: Leveraging cutting-edge research and technology for sustainable development
    4. Collaboration: Building strategic partnerships across sectors and borders
    5. Impact: Delivering measurable, sustainable change in mobility systems

    4. OUR TEAM

Evelyn Gathua – Founder & Managing Director
Evelyn Gathua is an environmental and sustainable mobility professional with expertise in transport policy and climate action. She holds a Bachelor of Environmental Studies in Resource Conservation from Kenyatta University (Kenya) and is a Licensed NEMA Associate Expert, complemented by specialized training in, Climate Governance, Project Management, and Environmental, Social & Governance from global institutions.
She has served in key roles as Strategy & Sustainability Manager at Sustainable Transport Africa, and Public Policy Assistant at Bolt. She has also provided expert consultancy services to prominent organizations including the World Resources Institute (WRI), where she led the groundbreaking Drive Electric Study, and the United Nations Environment Programme (UNEP), where she developed methodologies for assessing transport digitization and electric vehicles readiness in Africa.
Evelyn actively contributes to policy development through her membership in the Kenya Bureau of Standards Electric Mobility Committee (KEBS TC 199), the International Association for Impact Assessment (IAIA), and the Environment Institute of Kenya (EIK). Her expertise spans sustainable mobility research, environmental policy, and strategic leadership, demonstrated through her roles as a delegate at the Rethinking Transport Lab 2023 by GIZ, keynote speaker at GrowUp Demo Day 2023, and session lead at the Women & Transport Conference. She has collaborated with various international organizations including GIZ, UNEP, Siemens Stiftung, and Greenpeace Africa, focusing on electric mobility, sustainable transport solutions, and environmental conservation initiatives.

 Anthony Ndolo – Co-founder & Director of Strategy
Anthony Ndolo is a sustainable mobility innovator with expertise in technology-driven solutions for emerging markets. He holds a BSc in Applied Statistics with Computing from Maasai Mara University (Kenya), Software Engineering certification from ALX, complemented by specialized training in Investment Management by ANDE, Google Data Analytics and AI Career Essentials. He is also a Clarity4D accredited facilitator.
He has previously served in leadership roles including Programmes Manager at Westerwelle Foundation, COO at ECOWORLD RECYCLING and as founder and CEO of SMATBEBA. At ECOWORLD, he led sustainable development initiatives along the Kenyan Coast. While at SMATBEBA, he pioneered innovative cargo distribution solutions across East Africa, achieving over $100,000 in Annual Recurring Revenue and creating more than 10,000 job opportunities.
Anthony has participated in numerous international development initiatives, working with organizations such as GIZ, Shell Foundation, USAID, and various government agencies. His work spans sustainable mobility, environmental conservation, and digital transformation projects. As a 2022 Westerwelle Foundation Fellow and winner of the Africa Mobility Initiative grant of $25000, he has contributed to significant mobility and environmental projects across the region. His expertise has been recognized through his selection as a Greentech Africa Mobility Delegate to Bits & Pretzels, as a panelist at the MIT Africa Innovate Conference, and as one of the Digital Africa 1000 Winners representing Africa at the 2020 Africa-France Summit.
